The Five Phases

Markets move in cycles. This is not theory‚Äîit's observable fact, repeated across every asset class, every timeframe, every market condition. The names change. The structure doesn't.

Pentarch maps five distinct phases:

Touchdown TD

What it means: Selling exhaustion. The market has been falling, sellers are depleted, and accumulation is quietly building. This is the early reversal signal at cycle lows‚Äîthe moment when fear peaks but the bottom is forming.

Ignition IGN

What it means: The uptrend has sparked. Momentum shifts upward with conviction. This confirms that accumulation is complete and the markup phase has begun. Buyers are in control.

Warning WRN

What it means: Hidden weakness in rising prices. The market is still going up, but the internal structure is deteriorating. Distribution is occurring‚Äîselling into the strength while retail chases. This is the signal most traders miss.

Climax CAP

What it means: Buying exhaustion. The top is forming. Volume spikes as late buyers rush in, but distribution absorbs it all. Euphoria peaks. The ceiling has been reached.

Breakdown BDN

What it means: The downtrend begins. Structure breaks. The markup phase is definitively over, and gravity reclaims what greed lifted. Sellers take control.

Why This Matters

Knowing where you are in the cycle changes everything about how you trade.

If you're in TD (accumulation): You don't sell. You wait for IGN confirmation, then look for entries. The risk-reward is optimal here‚Äîyou're buying during accumulation, at prices the market is willing to support.

If you're in WRN (distribution): You don't buy breakouts. That "strength" is a trap. Distribution is selling into it. You either exit longs, tighten stops, or start looking for short setups.

If you're in CAP (climax): You definitely don't buy. This is where retail gets destroyed‚Äîchasing the top, buying the euphoria, holding through the reversal. Pentarch shows you the ceiling before you hit it.
