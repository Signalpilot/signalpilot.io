RISK

Position Sizing 101: Risk per Trade & R‑multiples

If your position size is a vibe, your P&L will be too. Use fixed % risk and size by stop distance so one trade can’t sink the ship.

Key ideas

  • Risk per trade: 0.5–2.0% of account is typical. Pick a number you can survive.
  • R‑multiple: Profit or loss relative to your risk. +2R means 2× your planned risk.
  • Stops first, size second: Entry minus stop defines risk per unit. Size = (max loss) / (risk per unit).

Worked example

  1. 1.
    Account $10,000. Risk 1% ⇒ $100 max loss.
  2. 2.
    Entry $100, stop $95 ⇒ risk per unit $5.
  3. 3.
    Position size = 100 / 5 = 20 units.
  4. 4.
    Target $110 ⇒ reward per unit $10 ⇒ R:R = 2:1. If hit: +2R = $200.
